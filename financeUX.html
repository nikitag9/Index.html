Finance UX at The Home Depot

My challenge on the Finance UX team was to reconstruct the Comparable Sales Report that was reported on the 10K and 10q in the quarterly report out to Wall Street. The current report had a very manual process of importing sales figures and many pain points from our business user which included heavy report load time, difficult user interface, and unautomated inputs. We needed to make these changes to reduce potential risks to the company as a result of misinformation through the manual process as well as improve this business process as a whole so that less time needed to be spent on it.

My first step was to conduct User Interviews on everyone involved in this business process which included the business user that was using the report as well as the IT team that managed it. By forming a cohesive script and asking questions related to these and more pain points and the process of using the report as whole, I was able to gain valuable insight to help me solve these issues. I then created a journey map of this business process so that our team could have a shared understanding of the process and associated pain points.

I spoke to the IT team regarding some of the pain points that he had and came up with solutions that either me and the Software Engineering interns could work on or longer-term solutions for the IT team to work on.â€‹ We reviewed various software that we could move the report to and decided that Tableau worked the best for our user's need for flexibility and to be able to create his own specialised view. We also found a critical process that takes a few hours multiple times a day during the critical report close period that we found a solution to reduce by 50%. We then conducted user testing by showing him a prototype of this report and getting his feedback on functionality and easy of use.
